Site Report



After a month of intense procrastination and artistic challenges, it feels great to say to myself, “Finally, it’s over”. As much as I love learning new aspects and methods of coding, the relief of finally completing an assignment is much more satisfactory. This was my first time building a website on my own from complete scratch and it was an amazing experience. I messed around with different elements, tags and syntaxes and learned so much about web development. There were few slip-ups here and there, but it was liberating to figure out the solutions on my own with the help of the greatest invention of mankind, YouTube. Let’s take a look at my progress from the very beginning to the very end.

UI/UX Design

The first thing that I did before even creating an HTML file was wireframing. I made a rough layout in my notebook with a pencil and tried to recreate is digitally but I was not satisfied with how it looked on my screen so I went along with my notebook version of the wireframe. When I actually started coding the layout, it looked tacky and some parts required JavaScript as well. So, instead, I went along with spontaneous ideas that popped into my head, which turned out to be a lot harder than I thought because I am a very indecisive person and I kept on changing the layout time and again.

Lesson Learned: Don’t code without a proper UI Prototype.

Theme Choice

The main colors that I used for my website were Black and Green. I tried to go for a command prompt-esque look and used the font “Cascadia Code” in the beginning. However, “Genos” seemed like a better fit, so it made it to the final design. There are some hints of red, green, yellow and blue in other pages, especially in the “Portfolio Page” where I used Hogwarts houses as placeholder images. The font and border colors were inspired by the colors of the four Hogwarts houses. I love Harry Potter so it had to be a part of my website. Initially, I was leaning towards a pastel theme with lilac, baby blue and pink colors.
The inspiration behind this theme was the portfolio of Brittany Chiang. I came across her portfolio page as I was browsing through some websites, looking for an inspiration. I was awe-struck by the minimal yet intriguing design of the website. Even though our websites don’t resemble each other at all, I was really inspired and impressed by her work.

Initial Rough Theme Idea

Biggest Challenges

The biggest obstacle that I had to overcome while designing this website, aside from constant battles with procrastination, was not being allowed to use absolute positioning and script. Every YouTube video that I watched and, every blog that I read always mentioned either of those two things and it was tough to completely avoid using both. Adding the Hamburger Menu and icons were a pain as well. I avoided using the hamburger icon from “Font Awesome” since I wasn’t sure if that was allowed and used the menu bar from “Google Fonts” instead. Also, I used photo instead of font icons to represent social media sites icon.
Furthermore, I struggled with "perferctionism" and doubts regarding the UI design of the website. As a non-artistic yet critical person, it was challenging for me to come up with creative ideas and even more challenging to actually like them.

Discoveries

The best part of this entire project was discovering resourceful websites and even some stunning ones. I discovered Figma for prototyping, Awwwards for inspiration, CodingNepal for source codes and ideas and many more. I also came across many creative websites and my favorite one so far is Onyedika.xyz.
Aside from personal growth, I also got to know my classmates a lot better while helping each other out and complaining about the Hamburger Menu together. Watching people struggle over the same things as me put me at ease and gave me a sense of solidarity.
Oh, and the biggest lesson that I learned throughout this project was: If a code somehow works, do not touch it.

Git

Git and GitHub were a foreign concept to me. I had only heard about them but never had to actually use it. I was a bit lost and confused the first time and uploaded my files via drag and drop as I was not aware of the complications it could possibly cause. I abandoned that repository and created a new one entitled "Portfolio" and learned how to commit through GitBash. However, I wasn't well aware about the frequency of commits, so I only committed my code when there was a huge change. So, I only had about 3 - 4 commits in about 3 weeks. I started committing more frequently after I realised the amount of commits that I had wasn't even close to enough.
